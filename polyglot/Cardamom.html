Cognates are useful.

But beware of false cognates

Sometimes mistakes are revealed and need to researched and corrected, when the tree generated from an Indo-European root includes, out in some branch, another Indo-European root. For example, at the time of writing, ten-.

Pathological situations can arise where the tree of descendents for an Indo-European root contains words which are entirely an amalgam of suffixes and prefixes, with nothing of the original PIE root remaining. e.g. (PIE) tḱey- → (la) pōnō → (en) compose, and (PIE) gʰed- → (en) prison. Fortunately these instances are rare.

Bunadas works with dictionary headwords (plus some supletive forms), rather than being “polluted” with wordforms like Wiktionary.

It is important to be rigorous regarding the canonical form to be used for headwords, and Bunadas usually follows Wiktionary. e.g. for verbs:

  • Greek: 1sg
  • Latin: 1sg
  • French: infinitive
  • German: infinitive
  • Welsh: infinitive (verbal-noun?)
  • Old Irish: 3sg
  • Irish: imperitive
      (compare: Dineen 1sg; EID 1sg; FGB imperitive; ING 3sg)
  • Gàidhlig: imperitive

Spelling standards are important too. Bunadas uses:

  • Gàidhlig: Am Faclair Beag (rather than GOC or Dwelly)
  • Old Irish: hyphens after verb prefixes like in DIL, but will move at some stage to middle-dot like Wiktionary
  • Irish: Caighdeán Oifigiúil, but would dearly like to somehow include Dineen

Maybe Bunadas should have a concept of alternative spelling systems – e.g. Gàidhlig (GOC/non-GOC); Irish (CO/Dineen); Serbian (sr-Cyrl/sr-Latn) ?

Would be nice to include lots more languages. But that needs a colour editor (at least for me).

Would be nice to be able to do things like “filter out all Balto-Slavic” from the trees. But Bunadas language codes are currently atomic and Bunadas does not have much understanding of a tree of languages.

Would be nice to include lots of etymology for placenames, family names, species names, etc, but this would require flagging, and filtering facilities. The language code for proper names like this is often very problematic.

Difficulties with the (lack of) data structure in Wiktionary. e.g. Start with English bole and try to go back to PIE.

Attempts to harvest etymological data automatically from Wiktionary:

  • Rabbitique
  • Etymologeek (stopped working?)
  • Etymological Wordnet by Gerard de Melo – See Lexvo

In the end, I believe everything will be derived from Lexicographical Wikidata – See Ordia.
But currently it has no concept of ≽ or ≫ – e.g. See palmist,
and no proper disambiguation – e.g. See ballpoint pen, and no consistency (?) as to whether homographs get different L-numbers (e.g. tear) or not (e.g. pen).
